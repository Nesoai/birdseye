Overlay districts & special conditions

  • (E) designation: hazmat and noise flags recorded on the lot from the district rezoning. Requires an OER-approved testing workplan (Phase I/II) and a Notice to Proceed before DOB issues permits — typically 2–4 months, run in parallel with design.
  • Parking: no residential parking minimum applies following the City of Yes zoning changes — the program can carry zero structured parking, a major cost saver on this lot size.
  • Flood: lot is outside the mapped 1%-annual-chance floodplain on the preliminary FIRMs; nearby waterfront blocks are in (see Report 2 for the full screen).
  • Landmarks: no designated landmark or historic district on the block — no LPC review trigger.

The entitlement path — which approvals, in what order

  1. Zoning confirmation (ZD1) with a code consultant — lock the as-of-right reading of FAR, height, and the MX use mix before hard money. ~1–2 weeks.
  2. OER workplan for the (E) designation — Phase I/II testing scope, remedial measures if triggered, Notice to Proceed. Runs parallel to design.
  3. DOB New Building filing — plan examination and objections cycle; a code/expediting consultant keeps rounds short. No public hearing.
  4. DOT / Builders Pavement Plan — curb cut and loading berth on the side street; ministerial with the DOB filing.
  5. Permit & construction sign-offs — ministerial once OER and plan exam clear.

Because the program is as-of-right, the risk here is administrative, not political: the two live items are (1) the (E) designation workplan — testing results set the remediation scope and schedule — and (2) DOB objection cycles during plan exam. Neither involves a community board vote or public hearing. The schedule lever is sequencing: file the OER workplan during due diligence so clearance lands alongside the DOB approval, not after it.
